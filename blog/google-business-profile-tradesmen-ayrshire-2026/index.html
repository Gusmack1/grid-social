Google Business Profile — Ayrshire Guide

The complete guide to Google Business Profile for tradesmen in Ayrshire (2026)

How to set up, optimise, and manage your Google Business Profile to get more calls from local customers — for free.

Published March 2026  ·  Grid Social
TL;DR: Google Business Profile (GBP) is a free listing that makes your business show up on Google Maps and in local search results. For tradespeople in Ayrshire, it's the single most effective thing you can do to get more enquiries. This guide covers exactly how to set it up and get the most out of it.

What is Google Business Profile?

Google Business Profile (previously called Google My Business) is your free listing on Google. When someone searches "plumber Kilmarnock" or "electrician near me", Google shows a map with three local businesses — the Local Pack. That's powered by Google Business Profiles.

If your business isn't listed, or your listing is incomplete, you simply don't show up. Competitors with complete, active profiles rank above you — even if you've been trading longer and do better work.

Step 1: Claim or create your profile

Go to business.google.com and search for your business name. If it already exists (Google often creates partial listings automatically), click "Claim this business." If it doesn't exist yet, click "Add your business."

You'll need a Google account. If you don't have one, create one using your business email address.

Step 2: Verify your business

Google needs to verify you're a real, local business. For most tradespeople, this means receiving a postcard with a 5-digit code sent to your business address. This takes 5–10 days. There are sometimes faster options (phone or video verification) — choose whichever Google offers you.

Important: Your verification address doesn't have to be shown publicly. If you work from home, you can hide your address and just show your service area — Ayrshire, or specific towns.

Step 3: Complete your profile properly

This is where most tradespeople stop short. A half-filled profile ranks poorly. Here's what to complete:

  • Business name — use your real trading name exactly. Don't keyword-stuff (e.g. "John Smith Plumber Kilmarnock" — just "John Smith Plumbing").
  • Category — choose the most accurate primary category (e.g. "Plumber", "Electrician", "General contractor"). Add secondary categories where relevant.
  • Service area — list all towns you cover. Don't just say "Ayrshire" — add Kilmarnock, Ayr, Troon, Irvine, and every specific town you work in.
  • Phone number — use your main number. Make sure it's correct — this is where the calls come in.
  • Opening hours — set accurate hours. If you offer emergency call-outs, add this in the description.
  • Description — write 150–300 words about what you do, where you work, and what makes you reliable. Include your key towns and services naturally.
  • Services — list every service you offer (e.g. boiler installation, bathroom fitting, emergency repairs). Google uses this to match your profile to relevant searches.

Step 4: Upload photos

Profiles with photos get significantly more clicks and calls than those without. Upload at least 10 photos to start:

  • Before and after shots of completed jobs
  • Your van or equipment (shows you're a real, professional business)
  • You or your team on a job (builds trust)
  • Any certificates or accreditations

Add new photos every month. Google rewards active profiles with better rankings.

Step 5: Get Google reviews

Reviews are the most powerful ranking factor in local search. A business with 20 five-star reviews will almost always outrank one with none — regardless of how long they've been trading.

The simplest approach: after every completed job, send your customer a WhatsApp message saying "Really glad you're happy with the work. If you have 2 minutes, a Google review would mean a lot — here's the link: [your Google review link]."

To get your review link: in your GBP dashboard, click "Get more reviews" and copy the short link. Save it on your phone so you can send it quickly after every job.

Step 6: Post regular Google updates

Google lets you post updates directly to your Business Profile — photos, job updates, offers, seasonal reminders. Businesses that post regularly rank better than those that don't. Aim for at least 2 posts per month. Keep them short: a photo, 2–3 sentences, and a call to action ("Call us for a free quote").

Common mistakes Ayrshire tradespeople make

  • Not verifying the profile — unverified profiles don't rank
  • Wrong or outdated phone number — you're paying for visibility but missing the calls
  • No service area set — Google doesn't know where to show you
  • No photos — customers won't call a business with no evidence of their work
  • Never posting updates — Google treats inactive profiles as less relevant
  • Not responding to reviews — even a simple "Thanks very much" shows you're engaged
