5 free tools every Ayrshire
small business should use.

You don't need a marketing budget to start getting found online. These five tools are completely free and take less than an hour to set up.

Published March 2026  ·  5 min read  ·  Grid Social
TL;DR: Google Business Profile, Canva, Meta Business Suite, Google Analytics, and WhatsApp Business are the five free tools every Ayrshire small business should be using. They cost nothing, take minutes to set up, and cover 90% of what you need to get found online.

1. Google Business Profile

If you only do one thing from this list, do this. Google Business Profile is the single most impactful free tool for any local business. When someone searches "plumber Kilmarnock" or "builder near me" on Google, the businesses that appear in the map results all have a Google Business Profile. If you don't have one, you're invisible to anyone searching for your trade in your area.

It takes about 15 minutes to set up. You add your business name, what you do, where you cover, your opening hours, and some photos of your work. Google then verifies you (usually by postcard) and within a few weeks you're showing up in local search results. We wrote a complete step-by-step guide if you want the full walkthrough.

2. Canva

Canva is a free design tool that lets you create professional-looking social media posts, flyers, business cards, and more — without any design experience. It has thousands of templates you can customise with your own text, colours, and photos. If you've ever wanted to create a "job completed" post for Facebook that looks polished rather than just a raw phone photo with a caption, Canva is how you do it.

The free version is more than enough for most small businesses. You can resize designs for different platforms, save your brand colours, and create templates you reuse every week. It's available on desktop and mobile.

3. Facebook Business Page

A Facebook Business Page is free and acts as your digital shopfront for the millions of people who use Facebook daily. For tradespeople in Ayrshire, it's especially powerful because of local community groups — when someone in "Kilmarnock Buy Sell & Swap" asks for a plumber recommendation, your business page is what people link to.

Make sure your page has a clear description of what you do and where you cover, recent photos of your work, your email address, and your opening hours. Post two or three times a week — job completions, tips, and behind-the-scenes content work best.

4. Google Analytics

If you have a website — even a simple one — Google Analytics tells you exactly how many people are visiting, where they're coming from, and what pages they're looking at. It's completely free and takes about 10 minutes to set up. You add a small piece of code to your website (or ask whoever built your site to do it), and Google starts tracking visitor data immediately.

This matters because it tells you whether your online efforts are actually working. If you start posting on social media and see a spike in website visitors from Facebook — you know it's working. If nobody's visiting from Google, you know your SEO needs attention. Data beats guesswork.

5. WhatsApp Business

WhatsApp Business is a free app that turns your regular WhatsApp into a professional communication tool. You can set up a business profile with your description and hours, create quick reply templates for common questions ("Thanks for your enquiry, I'll get back to you within 24 hours"), and set automated greeting messages so customers get an instant response even when you're on a job.

For tradespeople, this is particularly useful because most customer communication happens over WhatsApp anyway. Having the business version just makes it look more professional and saves you time with canned responses.

The bottom line

None of these tools cost a penny. They take less than an hour to set up in total. And together, they give any Ayrshire small business a solid online foundation — you're findable on Google, you look professional on social media, you can communicate efficiently with customers, and you know whether your efforts are paying off.
